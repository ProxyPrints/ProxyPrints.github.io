## Interpretation

Two real, load-bearing findings, not assumed going in:

1. **Over a third of this huge pool (34.3%) doesn't need Part 4's own
   distinguishing machinery at all** — plain collector-line OCR (already
   uncapped, already existed) resolves it. These cards are sitting
   unresolved for some other reason (not yet reached by the main pilot's
   own OCR pass, most likely, given OCR was never blocked by the cap) -
   Part 4's OCR-as-normal step 1 catches them for free as a side effect
   of building this module, not because artist-decomposition was
   needed.
2. **Artist-decomposition's own yield is real but modest: ~1.7% of the
   sample (5/300), against a ~16.0% "tried and came up ambiguous" cost**
   (artist extracted, phash then failed). The bottleneck isn't artist
   extraction itself (18.0% extraction rate is a reasonable real-world
   OCR yield) - it's that even a successfully artist-narrowed candidate
   set is often still too large or too visually similar for phash to
   confidently pick a unique winner. Scaled to the full pool: roughly
   39,707 × 5/300 ≈ **662 real votes** from artist-decomposition
   specifically (extrapolated from a 300-card sample, not a guarantee),
   on top of whatever the free OCR-resolved fraction (~34.3% × 39,707 ≈
   13,633 cards) would separately pick up (both figures are a linear extrapolation from a 300-card sample, not a guarantee).
